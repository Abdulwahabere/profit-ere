Bayern munich

FC Bayern Munich is a German sports club based in Munich, Bavaria. It is best known for its professional football team, which plays in the Bundesliga, the top tier of the German football league system, and is the most successful club in German football history, having won a record 30 national titles and 20 national cups.[1] Founded in 1900 by eleven football players led by Franz John,[2] the club had its period of greatest success in the middle of the 1970s when, under the captaincy of Franz Beckenbauer, they won the European Cup three consecutive seasons (1974–1976). Overall, Bayern has reached eleven European Cup/UEFA Champions League finals, most recently winning their sixth title in 2020 as part of a continental treble. Bayern has also won one UEFA Cup, one European Cup Winners' Cup, one UEFA Super Cup, one FIFA Club World Cup and two Intercontinental Cups, making it one of the most successful European clubs internationally. Borussia monchengladbach

Borussia Mönchengladbach was founded on August 1, 1900, by 13 young men who broke away from a previous sports club. The name "Borussia" is the Latin word for Prussia, as the city of Mönchengladbach was part of the Prussian kingdom at the time. The club experienced moderate success before a "golden era" in the 1970s under coach Hennes Weisweiler, which earned them the nickname "The Foals" for their attacking style and led to multiple titles, including the Bundesliga championship and the UEFA Cup.

RB Leipzig

RB Leipzig was founded in 2009 by Red Bull, which acquired the playing license of a fifth-tier team, SSV Markranstädt. The club rapidly ascended through the leagues, reaching the Bundesliga in 2016, and has since become a regular in the top flight, achieving significant success on the pitch, including reaching the Champions League semi-finals and winning consecutive DFB Pokal titles. Due to the club's ownership structure and perceived circumvention of traditional German football rules, it has faced criticism and protest from other fan bases.
